# {{title}}

- Iron/chromium redox flow battery (neg side:  Cr3+/Cr2+  ; pos side: Fe3+/Fe2+; exchange: H+ or Cl-)
- Vanadium redox flow battery (neg side: V3+/V2+ ; pos side: VO^2+/VO_2^+ ; exchange: H+ ?)

Note: supporting electrolyte (e.g. HCl) is usually there in excess
E.g. 1M of active redox and 3M of HCl.
- Acidity suppresses hydroxides formation.
- Cl complexation of chromium aides the kinetics.
- Note because supporting electrolyte is in excess then its ln(activity) doesn't change much.
