## Vacuum reference - "4.44 V"

There are a variety of definitions but let's talk about the widely-cited and poorly understood value "4.44 V".

This is often described as a more physical and fundamental reference to "the vacuum", however, ultimately it is a surface property akin to the work function.

Like the work function, the actual vacuum-referenced electrode potential varies a great deal with surface contamination and other factors.

The original measurements were done by Farrell and McTigue^[Farrell 1982] then interpreted by Trasatti to produce the widely cited value of "4.44 V".^[Trasatti 1984]

Note there is a little trick in Farrell & McTigue: to determine the position of "the SHE" they assign half of mean activity coefficient to each ion!

Hees and Zhang^[https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.jpclett.4c02923] include a careful discussion of how the meaning of absolute electrode potential is related to "ionic work function", and their work investigates molecular simulations of how the vacuum potential appears (relative to the SHE or the inner 'true' potential).
